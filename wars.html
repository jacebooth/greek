The Titanomachy was a ten-year cosmic battle between the mighty Titans and the emerging Olympians, led by Zeus. Cronus, the King of the Titans, feared a prophecy that one of his children would overthrow him, and he had devoured them at birth. Zeus, hidden by his mother Rhea, grew in secret and later freed his siblings, Hades and Poseidon. With the aid of the hundred-handed Hecatoncheires and the powerful Cyclopes, the young Olympians challenged the rule of the Titans. After a decade of fierce battles across earth, sky, and sea, Zeus and his allies triumphed. The defeated Titans were cast into Tartarus, bound forever by divine chains, and the Olympians established themselves as the new rulers of the cosmos.

The war of the Seven Against Thebes was fought when seven champions marched to attack the city of Thebes, seeking to restore Polynices to the throne. Led by Adrastus, each hero targeted a different gate of the city, bringing immense destruction. The battle ended in tragedy: Polynices and his brother Eteocles killed each other in combat, fulfilling a cursed prophecy that had plagued their family. This war highlighted the fragile nature of human ambition, divine intervention, and the inevitable consequences of pride and fate.
