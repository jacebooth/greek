The Titans of Greek Mythology

Cronus

King of the Titans and god of time. Cronus overthrew Uranus but was later overthrown by his son Zeus after devouring his own children to avoid prophecy.

Rhea

Queen of the Titans and mother of the Olympians. She saved Zeus by hiding him and tricking Cronus.

Oceanus

Titan of the world-encircling river. Father of river gods and ocean nymphs.

Tethys

Titaness of fresh water and nurturing rivers, wife of Oceanus.

Hyperion

Titan of heavenly light and observation, father of Helios, Selene, and Eos.

Theia

Titaness of shining light and precious metals, mother of celestial bodies.

Iapetus

Titan of mortality and craftsmanship, father of Prometheus and Atlas.

Mnemosyne

Titaness of memory and language, mother of the nine Muses.

Phoebe

Titaness of prophecy and intellect, grandmother of Apollo and Artemis.

Coeus

Titan of intellect and the axis of heaven, associated with foresight.

Crius

Titan of constellations and cosmic order, linked to the stars.

Atlas

Condemned to hold up the sky for eternity after the Titanomachy.

Prometheus

Titan who defied Zeus by giving fire to humanity. Punished by eternal torment.

Epimetheus

Brother of Prometheus, known for accepting Pandora and unleashing suffering upon mankind.

Metis

Titaness of wisdom and strategy. Swallowed by Zeus to prevent prophecy; mother of Athena.
